# 📝 ScanWise Tone of Voice & Content Guidelines

## Philosophy
**"Supportive Friend, Not Medical Lecture"**

Every word in ScanWise should make users feel:
- ✅ Capable, not intimidated
- ✅ Supported, not judged
- ✅ Empowered, not scared
- ✅ Part of a community, not alone

---

## 🎯 Core Principles

### **1. Encouraging, Never Judgmental**

| ❌ Judgmental | ✅ Supportive |
|--------------|---------------|
| "You chose a product high in sugar." | "This product has more sugar than your goal. No worries—here are better options!" |
| "This contains your allergen." | "This isn't safe for you, but I found 3 alternatives that are!" |
| "You're not tracking properly." | "You've scanned 5 products—great start! Keep going!" |
| "Avoid this at all costs." | "This might not be ideal for your diabetes goal, but it's not terrible." |
| "Medical attention required." | "Talk to your doctor about this—here's how to share with them." |

---

### **2. Clear, Not Condescending**

| ❌ Too Technical | ✅ Plain English |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| "Glycemic Index: 65" | "This will likely spike your blood sugar" |
| "HIPAA Consent Disclosure" | "Your health information is private and only doctors can see it" |
| "Macronutrient ratio imbalance" | "Good mix of protein, carbs, and healthy fats" |
| "Albumin glycation indicator" | "Product helps with your diabetes goal" |
| "Cross-contamination risk assessment" | "Small chance of traces from other allergens" |
| "Sodium: 480mg (24% DV)" | "This is 24% of your daily salt limit" |

---

### **3. Celebratory, Not Dismissive**

| ❌ Dismissive | ✅ Celebratory |
|-------------|-----------------|
| "First scan complete" | "🎉 YOU DID IT! Great work! 👏" |
| "5 products scanned" | "You've made 5 informed choices this month!" |
| "Incident reported" | "Thank you for helping your community! Your report will save lives." |
| "Profile created" | "Welcome to ScanWise! You're ready to make confident health decisions." |

---

### **4. Offer Choices, Not Rules**

| ❌ Commanding | ✅ Offering |
|-------------|-----------|
| "You must consent." | "We need your consent to proceed. Want to learn more?" |
| "You need to enter your A1C." | "Want to enter your A1C? Leave blank if unsure." |
| "Read the privacy policy." | "Want to see our privacy policy? It's worth a read!" |
| "Share your experience." | "Help others? You can share your experience anytime." |

---

### **5. Present, Not Absent**

| ❌ Disappeared | ✅ Always Here |
|--------------|-----------------|
| [No error message] | "Oops! Barcode not recognized. Try pointing steadier, or search by product name." |
| [Silent failure] | "Something went wrong. We're on it! In the meantime, try scanning again." |
| [Empty state] | "No scans yet! Ready to try your first one? →" |

## 🎤 Voice & Tone Reference

### **Use When:**
- **Celebratory** → User completes milestone (first scan, 10 scans, badge earned)
- **Encouraging** → User is stuck or confused
- **Supportive** → User shares incident report
- **Educational** → Explaining features or health concepts
- **Empowering** → Showing progress or alternatives
- **Friendly** → General communication

### **Avoid When:**
- ❌ Being dismissive ("This is easy")
- ❌ Using jargon without explanation
- ❌ Demanding compliance
- ❌ Making assumptions
- ❌ Being alarmist
- ❌ Disappearing in errors

## 📝 Specific Writing Rules

### **Rule 1: Lead with Impact**
```
❌ "This product has 12g of sugar"
✅ "This might spike your blood sugar"
```

### **Rule 2: Give Context Immediately**
```
❌ "A1C: 7.2"
✅ "Your A1C: 7.2 (target: under 7)"
```

### **Rule 3: Offer Solutions, Not Just Problems**
```
❌ "Too much sodium"
✅ "This is high in sodium. Try these lower-sodium options instead: →"
```

### **Rule 4: Use "You/Your" Not "The User"**
```
❌ "The user's blood sugar will spike"
✅ "Your blood sugar will likely spike"
```

### **Rule 5: Numbers Should Have Meaning**
```
❌ "Glycemic Load: 16"
✅ "Glycemic Load: 16 (moderate impact on blood sugar)"
```

### **Rule 6: Always Offer Next Steps**
```
❌ [Shows result, no next action]
✅ "Here are better options ↓" or "Ready to try another scan? →"
```

## 🎯 Scenario-Specific Language

### **Onboarding**
- **Tone:** Warm, inviting, no pressure
- **Goal:** Make user feel welcome and capable
- Examples:
  - "Let's get to know you..."
  - "No wrong answers here!"
  - "You can change this anytime"
  - "Ready? Let's get started!"

### **First Scan**
- **Tone:** Encouraging, step-by-step, celebratory
- **Goal:** Build confidence
- Examples:
  - "Let's try it together!"
  - "You did it! 🎉"
  - "Great job on your first scan!"
  - "Now let's understand what this means..."

### **Education**
- **Tone:** Clear, accessible, not condescending
- **Goal:** Build knowledge without overwhelming
- Examples:
  - "Here's the simple version..."
  - "Want to know more? Tap to expand"
  - "This matters because..."
  - "Think of it like this..."

### **Community Reports**
- **Tone:** Real, relatable, validating
- **Goal:** Show users aren't alone
- Examples:
  - "14 people with diabetes had the same reaction"
  - "Here's what Maria experienced..."
  - "You're not alone in this"
  - "Your community recommends..."

### **Incident Reporting**
- **Tone:** Supportive, thank-you-focused, not alarmist
- **Goal:** Encourage sharing without dramatizing
- Examples:
  - "Thank you for helping your community!"
  - "Your experience will save lives"
  - "This helps others recognize early warning signs"
  - "Doctors will verify your report"

### **Alerts & Warnings**
- **Tone:** Urgent but not panicked, actionable
- **Goal:** Motivate action without fear
- Examples:
  - "🚨 CALL 911 NOW" (for emergencies only)
  - "⚠️ CAUTION - This might not be safe"
  - "This contains your allergen" (matter-of-fact, not scary)

## 🎨 Emoji Usage Guidelines

### **Use Emoji When:**
- ✅ Celebrating milestones (🎉, 🏆, 👏, 🌟)
- ✅ Indicating safety (✅, 🚨, ⚠️, 🔴, 🟢)
- ✅ Explaining concepts (❤️ for heart, 🩺 for diabetes, 🚫 for allergens)
- ✅ Encouraging action (→, 👉, 📱)
- ✅ Building warmth (😊, 💪, 💚)

### **Avoid Emoji When:**
- ❌ Professional medical information
- ❌ Serious warnings (use instead of emoji, not alongside)
- ❌ Formal reports or data
- ❌ More than 1-2 per message (not overwhelming)

## ✅ Content Checklist Before Publishing

Before any message goes live, ask:

- [ ] Does it make users feel capable?
- [ ] Is it free of jargon? (Or explained in plain English?)
- [ ] Does it offer a solution or next step?
- [ ] Is it encouraging, not judgmental?
- [ ] Would a non-tech person understand it?
- [ ] Does it feel like a supportive friend talking?
- [ ] Are we celebrating effort, not just results?
- [ ] Is there always a way to get more help?

---

## 🚀 Final Rule

**When in doubt, ask yourself:**

> "Would I say this to a friend managing this health condition?"

If the answer is no, rewrite it. ✍️
